Forms Protective Layer

Sunscreen forms a crucial protective layer on the skin, acting as a barrier against the harmful effects of the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays. This specialized layer shields the skin from both UVA and UVB rays, preventing them from penetrating into the deeper layers of the skin. UVA rays are responsible for premature aging, while UVB rays cause sunburn and can lead to skin cancer over time.

Daily Use

Daily use of sunscreen is a fundamental aspect of maintaining healthy and radiant skin. Whether the sun is shining brightly or hiding behind clouds, harmful UV rays are constantly present, ready to damage our skin. By applying sunscreen every day, we create a powerful shield against these UV rays, reducing the risk of sunburn, premature aging, and skin cancer. Sunscreen's protective barrier acts as a first line of defense, preventing the harmful rays from penetrating into the deeper layers of our skin

SPF and Skin Tone

The skin tone of sunscreen refers to the formulation's ability to blend seamlessly with different skin types and colors. Today, many sunscreens are designed to be suitable for various skin tones, ensuring that they do not leave a white or ashy cast on the skin, especially on individuals with darker complexions. Sunscreen manufacturers have recognized the importance of catering to diverse skin tones, and as a result, they have developed innovative formulas that offer transparent or tinted options.

For all Ages

Sunscreen is a product suitable for all ages, making it an essential part of sun protection for people of every generation. From infants to the elderly, everyone can benefit from using sunscreen to safeguard their skin from harmful UV rays.

Light Weight

With its feather-light texture, applying sunscreen becomes a breeze, effortlessly gliding onto the skin without leaving any heavy or greasy feeling behind. How To Apply Sunscreen

Amount Matters

tart by using an adequate amount of sunscreen. For the face and neck, a dollop about the size of a quarter (approximately 1.5 teaspoons) is generally recommended. For the entire body, you'll need about one ounce (approximately 30 milliliters) – equivalent to filling a shot glass.

Apply on ClearSkin

Before applying sunscreen, make sure your skin is clean and dry. Remove any makeup, dirt, or excess oil with a gentle cleanser. Pat your skin dry with a towel, leaving a slightly damp surface for better sunscreen absorption.

Thorough Application

Begin applying sunscreen evenly to your exposed skin. Start with your face, neck, and ears, making sure to cover all areas, including the often-neglected sides of the neck and behind the ears. Move on to your arms, legs, and any other exposed body parts. Don't forget areas like the back of your hands and the tops of your feet. Use gentle, circular motions to blend the sunscreen into your skin fully.

Reapply Regularly

Sunscreen effectiveness diminishes over time, especially after sweating, swimming, or towel-drying. Reapply sunscreen every two hours or more frequently if you're engaged in water-related activities. Even with water-resistant sunscreens, reapplication is necessary after about 40 to 80 minutes of water exposure.
